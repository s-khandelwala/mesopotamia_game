# 𒀭 Mesopotamia — An Educational Strategy Game

> _"Between the two rivers, civilisation was born."_

**Mesopotamia** is a browser-based strategy and simulation game set in ancient Sumer, circa 2800 BCE. Players experience life in one of history's earliest civilisations — either as a ruler building and governing a city-state, or as an ordinary citizen trying to survive the rhythms of daily life. Every mechanic is grounded in real historical research.

---

## Educational Purpose

This game is designed to support learning in:

- **Ancient History** — Mesopotamia, Sumer, the city of Uruk, early civilisation
- **Social Studies** — social hierarchy, economy, religion, taxation, governance
- **Geography** — the role of rivers (Tigris & Euphrates), irrigation, and agriculture in early societies
- **Critical Thinking** — resource management, decision-making under uncertainty, long-term planning

Historical facts are woven throughout the game: building descriptions reference real Sumerian practices, random events mirror documented disasters and opportunities, and the technology tree follows the actual order of Sumerian innovations.

---

## Game Modes

### 👑 Rule as Ensi — City Builder

Take the role of the **Ensi** (priest-governor) of a growing Sumerian city-state. Build on a grid map, manage your population, research technologies, and strive to construct the Great Ziggurat.

**Key concepts covered:**

- City planning and infrastructure (farms, wells, granaries, temples)
- Population management and specialisation (workers, scholars, soldiers)
- Resource economics: grain, water, silver, morale, intelligence, divine favour
- Technology progression (irrigation → writing → mathematics → metallurgy)
- Religious and political authority in ancient Sumer
- Military conflict and trade between city-states

**Win condition:** Complete all six civic Quests, grow your population to 150, and build the Great Ziggurat.

---

### 🌾 Live as a Citizen — Daily Survival

Play as a common citizen of Uruk — a **Farmer**, **Potter**, or **Merchant** — and manage your household day by day across multiple seasons and years.

**Key concepts covered:**

- Daily life and social class in ancient Sumer
- The temple economy: taxes, offerings, and religious obligation
- Seasonal agriculture and the vulnerability to floods, droughts, and plague
- Trade and craft specialisation
- Family structure and household management

**Choose your archetype:**

| Archetype   | Starting Bonus                | Special Skill                                               |
| ----------- | ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| 🌾 Farmer   | 18 grain, 5 silver            | Tend Irrigation — boosts the next 3 harvests                |
| 🏺 Potter   | 8 grain, 10 silver, 5 pottery | Kiln Batch — fire 6–10 vessels at once                      |
| ⚖️ Merchant | 6 grain, 18 silver            | Caravan Route — guaranteed profitable trade once per season |

**Goal:** Achieve your six **Legacy Goals** — four shared (Patriarch, Devout, Famous, Long-Lived) and two unique to your archetype — before your citizen's life ends.

## Features

- **Interactive Tutorial** for both game modes, with guided steps and highlighted UI elements
- **Historical lore popups** — the first time you use each action or place a building, a Scribe's Note with real historical context appears
- **Save system** — multiple named save slots with autosave; completed games are archived but cannot be reloaded
- **Seasonal events** — floods, droughts, plagues, bountiful harvests, desert raids, and more
- **Technology tree** — 10 researched technologies that unlock new buildings and production bonuses
- **Legacy system** — long-term, archetype-specific goals that reward thoughtful play over multiple years
- **Rotating historical facts** on the main menu, drawn from real scholarship on Sumerian civilisation

## Historical Notes

A selection of the real history embedded in the game:

| Topic                 | Historical Basis                                                                            |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Barley economy**    | Barley was the primary crop and currency of Sumer; workers were paid in daily beer rations  |
| **Temple as bank**    | Sumerian temples functioned as granaries, factories, courts, and financial institutions     |
| **Cuneiform writing** | Invented ~3200 BCE for accounting, not literature; pressed into wet clay with a reed stylus |
| **Corvée labour**     | Citizens owed compulsory labour to the state for canal and wall maintenance                 |
| **The Ziggurat**      | A massive stepped pyramid serving as the earthly home of the city's patron deity            |
| **City of Uruk**      | Likely the world's first true city (~80,000 people by 2900 BCE), credited to Gilgamesh      |
| **Trade networks**    | Lapis lazuli from Afghanistan, copper from Oman — caravans travelled 3,000 km               |
| **Temple taxes**      | Scribes kept meticulous clay tablet records; tax evasion meant severe punishment            |
